IMF — Briefing: Protocol Delta

Field agent, this encrypted briefing contains six sealed items from the archive. All have been anonymized — their truths must be coaxed out by observation and patience. Nothing is printed in plain type. Proceed carefully.

Item I — The Whispering Ledger

A ledger records a single sequence of fractions: the first page lists a single entry, the second page two entries, the third page three, and so on. The archived note reads: collect pages until the total sum of the ledger exceeds one point six nine. How many pages will the operative need to consult before the threshold is surpassed?

(The ledger's entries follow the pattern of unit fractions.)

Item II — The Blueprint Shrink

On the wall hangs a hexagonal insignia of the agency. An assistant marked midpoints on each edge and traced the inner figure that connects them. The assistant then whispered: the outer domain is N times the inner domain. Report the integer N the assistant meant.

(Both figures are regular and similar in orientation.)

Item III — The Three-Key Lock

From the gadget vault there are five colored chips. To arm the alarm you must pick a three-chip sequence and lay them in order. The technician's note forbids immediately repeating the same color. How many distinct arming sequences are possible?

(Order matters; repetition allowed except for consecutive identical chips.)

Item IV — The Dialed Cipher

A circular dial has three tumblers. The briefcase opens when the operator sets the number so that it leaves remainders 2, 3, and 4 when divided by 3, 5, and 7 respectively. The senior operative scribbled, smallest such setting. What integer did they write?

(Classic remainder constraints apply.)

Item V — The Echo Sequence

A training log begins with two signatures: 3 and 5. Each next signature is the sum of the two previous signatures. The sergeant asks: what number is written at the sixth signature?

(Start positions are given; standard additive recurrence.)

Item VI — The Paired Suits

In the mess, two cards were drawn from a deck for a quick wager. The underling wrote: both matched the same guild. For the bookkeeper's ledger they kept the numerator of the reduced chance of such a draw. What numerator did they note?

(A standard 52-card deck is implied; suits = guilds.)

All items are sealed. The archive keeper will provide the authorized decryption key (separately) to assemble the final access token.
